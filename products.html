Coin Days Destroyed or 'spent coin days' is a concept that might sound complex at first, but it's actually quite straightforward. It doesn't refer to the spending of coins in the usual sense. So, what exactly is being 'spent'? This concept revolves around the significance of the age of Bitcoins that have been held in wallets for a long periodâ€”specifically, those held for 155 days or more. It's like saying each Bitcoin held for a day earns 'one coin day'. When these Bitcoins are moved to a different wallet, it's as if those accumulated 'coin days' are spent or used up.

When we talk about high Coin Days Destroyed (CDD) data, it indicates that the Bitcoins being transferred have been held for a significant period before the move. It's important to understand that this metric isn't about the volume of BTC being transferred; it's about the time those Bitcoins have been held. In simpler terms, it's a way to measure the depth of commitment in the Bitcoin community, not just the quantity of transactions.
